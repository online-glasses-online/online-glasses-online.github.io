Understanding Age-Related Vision Changes

What is Presbyopia?

Presbyopia is a natural part of aging. It's the gradual loss of your eye's ability to focus on nearby objects. This happens because the lens inside your eye, responsible for focusing, loses its elasticity over time. As a result, it can no longer adjust its shape effectively to focus on close-up tasks like reading or sewing. This is why many women start needing readers eyeglasses womens in their 40s. The condition isn't a disease, but rather a change in the eye's physiology. Think of it like the natural wear and tear on any other part of your body.

Are you finding yourself holding reading material further and further away to make the words clearer? This is a common early symptom of presbyopia.
